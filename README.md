# Funnel and Revenue Analysis in Ecommerce

## Summary
The goal of this project is to analyze user behaviour across an ecommerce funnel and identify key drivers of user conversion and revenue in a 90-day period. The project focuses on the following:
- Funnel drop-off between stages
- Conversion perfromance by traffic source
- Revenue contribution by channel and product
- Time-to-conversion analysis

 ## Funnel and Revenue Analysis: Key Insights
 ### 1. The biggest dropoff is after Page View
 To isolate where users drop off in the funnel, I computed stage-level conversions using conditional aggregation:

	COUNT(DISTINCT CASE WHEN event_type = 'page_view' THEN user_id END) AS stage_1_views,
    COUNT(DISTINCT CASE WHEN event_type = 'add_to_cart' THEN user_id END) AS stage_2_cart,
    COUNT(DISTINCT CASE WHEN event_type = 'checkout_start' THEN user_id END) AS stage_3_checkout,
    COUNT(DISTINCT CASE WHEN event_type = 'payment_info' THEN user_id END) AS stage_4_payment,
    COUNT(DISTINCT CASE WHEN event_type = 'purchase' THEN user_id END) AS stage_5_purchase

This reveals that the biggest dropp-off comes right after the Page View event with 31% of the users leaving the site after viewing the products. This signals weak purchase intent or perhaps product-page friction. Conversion becomes stronger after checkout where 92% of users continue to purchase, suggesting that the issues are in the top funnel stages.

### 2. Email is the most effective channel
To evaluate the effectivenes of the traffic sources implemented by the company, I analized the ratios from the views generated by each source and the purchases derived from them
            
            ROUND(carts/views,2) AS cart_conversion_rate,
            ROUND(purchases/views,2) AS purchase_conversion_rate,
            ROUND(purchases/carts,2) AS cart_to_purchase_conversion_rate
            
The results show that email has the highers rate conversion from view to purchase with 34%, despite its low view volume. This suggests that the audience being targeted via email has high purchase intent. On the other hand, social media seems to bring the most viewers to the site, but it is underperforming in purchases. This is likely caused by poor targeting.

### 3. Most users convert within a 23-25 minute window
Most users convert within a tight 23-25 minute window on average, which shows a relatively short decision cycle with a low-consideration purchase behaviour. This window remains consistent throught the six weeks studied.
